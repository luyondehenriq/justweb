Forex Education

Forex Basics

Forex trading involves exchanging currencies, such as EUR/USD, GBP/JPY, and more. The forex market is the largest globally, operating 24 hours a day. Beginners should understand exchange rates, pips, lot sizes, and major trading sessions (London, New York, Tokyo).

Technical Analysis

Technical analysis uses historical price data to forecast market moves. Traders analyze candlestick charts, trends, and indicators like RSI, MACD, and moving averages to find entry and exit points.

Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis looks at economic indicators affecting currencies: GDP, inflation, interest rates, and employment data. Major news releases can cause sharp price movements, so staying informed is critical.

Risk Management

Risk management protects capital. Use stop-loss orders, determine risk per trade (1–2% of account), and avoid over-leveraging. Position sizing and discipline are crucial for long-term trading success.

Trading Psychology

Trading psychology deals with emotions like fear and greed. Stick to your plan, remain patient, avoid revenge trading, and maintain a trading journal to improve your skills.

Advanced Strategies

Advanced strategies include scalping (short-term trades), day trading, swing trading, and carry trades (profit from interest rate differences). Always back-test strategies in a demo account before trading live.
